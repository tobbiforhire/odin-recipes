Steak

A sizzling hot, juicy & tender cut of beef steak.

A quintessential dinner entree, beef steak is dish loved by many, apt for formal dining and familiar gatherings alike.

With many different ways to prepare it, there is a steak for all kinds of palates. This recipe, however, will yeild a classic 'steakhouse-style' medium-rare cut; serves one.

Ingredients:

  • 1 ribeye, t-bone, or sirloin steak (1.5-2" thick)
  • 2 tbsp high smoke point oil
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 cloves garlic left in skins
  • 1 bunch of fresh thyme
  • Kosher salt
  • Black pepper

Steps:

  1. Bring steak to room temperature
  2. Pour oil onto a cast iron skillet and heat to just below the oil smoking point
  3. While the skillet is heating, season the steak generously with salt and pepper on both sides
  4. Place steak on hot skillet and sear for 2-3 minutes on either side
  5. Add the butter, lightly crushed garlic cloves, and thyme to skillet
  6. Tilt the skillet and baste the steak with the butter until it reaches the internal temperature for desired doneness (135Â°F for medium rare)
  7. Once the steak reaches the desired doneness, immediately remove the steak from the pan onto a cutting board
  8. Immediately after, pour the butter with garlic and thyme on steak, and let rest 5 minutes
  9. Serve with desired sides and enjoy
